THE PROJECT

The Valerio Evangelisti Archive is a research project dedicated to the preservation, modeling, and valorization of the born-digital literary heritage of one of Italy's most important science fiction and historical fiction authors.

The project focuses on the born-digital partition of Evangelisti's archive, documenting the author's digital activity from the early 1990s through 2022. As a pioneer in computer adoption since the 1980s, Evangelisti consistently experimented with new technologies, integrating them into his creative and documentary practices.

The collection comprises materials from the desktop computer hard drive (documenting primary production environment), the external hard drive (backup and supplementary materials), and 93 digitized floppy disks from early creative periods. The project employs semantic web technologies and the Born-Digital Archival Ontology (BoDi), developed as an extension of Records in Contexts Ontology (RiC-O), to create a comprehensive knowledge graph representing files in their physical, logical, and content dimensions. Through integration of hash codes for integrity verification, native metadata extraction, and complete provenance documentation, the archive ensures long-term preservation and scholarly accessibility.

Through an interactive ResearchSpace platform, researchers and enthusiasts can navigate the entire digital production using advanced semantic queries, exploring thematic connections, temporal patterns, and transversal relationships that reveal the evolution of Evangelisti's narrative poetics across three decades of digital creativity.

METHODOLOGY

The project adopts a four-phase approach to modeling, managing, and publishing born-digital archival materials through semantic web technologies. The methodology takes as input the analysis of file system directories and produces as output a comprehensive knowledge base represented and accessible through multiple modalities: semantic queries via SPARQL endpoint, hierarchical navigation interfaces, network visualizations, and temporal analysis tools.
